Dylan's Genius

Author: Ishan Joshi

Date: 24/7/2018

I have been a Fan of Robert Zimmerman’s Music for a while now, considering he has now won a Nobel prize, I thought I would write an article on him(I know this article is too late for that). So, why do I think Bob Dylan’s a Genius? Well let’s compare two of my favorite songs by Him - Mr. Tambourine Man and Rainy Day Women #13&35. The theme of the song Mr. Tambourine Man is simple with a nice acoustic guitar in the background and Dylan’s rustic voice singing - “Hey! Mr Tambourine Man play a song for me I'm not sleepy and there ain't no place I'm going to” While the song Rainy Day Women is having the same acoustic guitar looping the same chords over and over, in sort of a psychedelic quality, in the background but the Lyrics start with - “Well, they'll stone you when you're trying to be so good They'll stone you just like they said they would” While the first song is about a fan singing about a Street Artist and the second song has the notion of drug abuse because of the recurring use of the word “Stoned”. So, here’s where it gets interesting. If one closely inspects Mr. Tambourine Man, the Lyrics say - “Take me on a trip upon your magic swirling ship My senses have been stripped May hands can't feel to grip My toes too numb to step” These lines suggest that the poet/singer is an drug addict and is having problems with his/her Drug Detoxification. On the other hand, the song Rainy Day Women has the lyrics - “They’ll stone ya when you’re trying to make a buck They’ll stone ya and then they’ll say “good luck” Tell ya what, I would not feel so all alone Everybody must get stoned ” Now, upon close inspection of these words, it suggests that the poet/singer is just trying to make it on his own, by not following the rules and not comprising on his dreams, and the world is trying to make him understand that his way to success is not right. Thereby, the word “Stoned” in this song would mean the World trying to make Dylan one of them. Hence, the first song is about battling drug addiction, while the second is about a Rebel and trying to find his own way in the world. This word play all while maintaining the beautiful rhymes is what makes Bob Dylan a Genius.
